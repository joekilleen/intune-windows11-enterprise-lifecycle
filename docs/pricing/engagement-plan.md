# Engagement Plan  
**Windows 11 Enterprise Device Lifecycle Implementation (Microsoft Intune)**

---

## 1. Purpose

This Engagement Plan defines **how the Statement of Work (SOW) will be executed**, including delivery phases, governance cadence, roles, and communication structure.

The objective is to:
- Provide predictability and transparency
- Align stakeholders on execution approach
- Reduce delivery risk
- Enable timely acceptance and closure

This plan governs **delivery execution**, not scope.

---

## 2. Delivery Model

### 2.1 Engagement Type

- Fixed-scope, fixed-fee engagement
- Remote-first delivery model
- Milestone-based execution and acceptance

---

### 2.2 Guiding Principles

- Security and compliance first
- Pilot before production
- Evidence-driven delivery
- No undocumented changes
- Clear ownership and escalation paths

---

## 3. Phased Delivery Approach

### Phase 0 — Initiation and Readiness

**Duration:** 3–5 business days

**Objectives:**
- Confirm scope and assumptions
- Establish access and roles
- Align on communication and governance

**Key Activities:**
- Kickoff meeting
- Access provisioning (least privilege)
- Stakeholder identification
- Finalize delivery schedule

**Deliverables:**
- Confirmed delivery plan
- Communication cadence
- Risk and dependency log

---

### Phase 1 — Architecture and Design

**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

**Objectives:**
- Define enterprise-grade architecture
- Establish design baselines

**Key Activities:**
- Requirements validation
- Architecture definition
- Security and compliance design
- Non-production and pilot strategy

**Deliverables:**
- Architecture documentation
- Assumptions and constraints
- High-level diagrams

---

### Phase 2 — Core Configuration Design

**Duration:** 2–3 weeks

**Objectives:**
- Define enforceable endpoint controls

**Key Activities:**
- Autopilot and ESP design
- Security baseline definition
- Compliance and access model
- Application lifecycle architecture
- Update and servicing strategy

**Deliverables:**
- Configuration documentation
- Policy mappings
- Validation criteria

---

### Phase 3 — Validation and Pilot

**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

**Objectives:**
- Validate designs in controlled environment
- Identify and remediate issues

**Key Activities:**
- Pilot group selection
- Policy validation
- Application testing
- Update ring validation
- Rollback verification

**Deliverables:**
- Pilot validation report
- Identified issues and resolutions
- Go/no-go recommendation

---

### Phase 4 — Governance, Operations, and Compliance

**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

**Objectives:**
- Enable sustainable operations
- Prepare for audit and scale

**Key Activities:**
- RBAC and scope tag design
- Access review process
- Lifecycle runbook development
- Compliance mapping
- Evidence framework definition

**Deliverables:**
- Operational runbooks
- Governance documentation
- Compliance crosswalk
- Evidence mapping matrix

---

### Phase 5 — Documentation, Handoff, and Acceptance

**Duration:** 1 week

**Objectives:**
- Finalize deliverables
- Enable client ownership
- Achieve acceptance

**Key Activities:**
- Documentation finalization
- Executive summary delivery
- One-page executive brief
- Acceptance walkthrough
- Final sign-off

**Deliverables:**
- Final documentation package
- Acceptance confirmation

---

## 4. Governance and Communication

### 4.1 Meeting Cadence

| Meeting | Frequency | Audience |
---|---|---|
| Status update | Weekly | Core team |
| Design review | As needed | Technical stakeholders |
| Executive update | Milestone-based | Leadership |
| Risk escalation | Ad hoc | Decision makers |

---

### 4.2 Reporting

Weekly status reports will include:
- Progress against plan
- Risks and issues
- Upcoming milestones
- Decisions required

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### Client Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
---|---|
| Executive Sponsor | Direction and risk acceptance |
| Technical Lead | Day-to-day coordination |
| Security | Review and approval |
| Operations | Validation and handoff |

---

### Provider Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
---|---|
| Engagement Lead | Overall delivery |
| Endpoint Architect | Architecture and design |
| Security Architect | Security and compliance |
| Documentation Lead | Deliverables and evidence |

---

## 6. Risk and Issue Management

- Risks are logged and reviewed weekly
- High-impact risks are escalated immediately
- Mitigations are documented
- Risk acceptance requires executive approval

---

## 7. Change Control

- All scope changes follow the Change Request process
- No informal or verbal changes are accepted
- Approved changes may affect timeline or pricing

---

## 8. Acceptance and Closure

- Acceptance governed by Acceptance Criteria document
- Deliverables reviewed per defined timelines
- Final acceptance triggers engagement closure

---

## 9. Assumptions and Dependencies

This plan assumes:
- Timely access and approvals
- Client participation in reviews
- Stable scope per SOW

Unmet assumptions may impact delivery.

---

## 10. Escalation Path

| Issue Type | Escalation |
---|---|
| Delivery risk | Engagement Lead |
| Security concern | Security Lead |
| Scope dispute | Executive Sponsor |
| Contractual issue | Legal / Procurement |

---

## 11. Summary

This Engagement Plan provides a **predictable, governed, and defensible delivery approach** aligned with enterprise expectations.

When followed:
- Risk is managed
- Scope is controlled
- Delivery is transparent
- Acceptance is smooth
